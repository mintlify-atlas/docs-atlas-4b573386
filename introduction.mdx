## What is Echo?

Echo is an anonymous micro-blogging platform designed specifically for college communities. Built with Next.js 15, it provides a safe space for students to share thoughts, connect with peers, and engage in meaningful conversations without revealing their identity.

## Technology stack

Echo is built with modern web technologies:

- **Framework**: Next.js 15 with App Router and React 19
- **Database**: PostgreSQL with Prisma ORM
- **Authentication**: NextAuth.js with credential-based auth
- **Styling**: Tailwind CSS with Radix UI components
- **State Management**: Zustand and TanStack Query
- **Rate Limiting**: Upstash Redis
- **Media Storage**: Vercel Blob
- **Content Safety**: TensorFlow.js with NSFWJS

## Why Echo?

College students often want to share thoughts, ask questions, or discuss topics without fear of judgment or identification. Echo provides:

- **True anonymity**: Unlike other platforms, Echo uses cryptographic hashing to ensure your identity remains private
- **College-specific**: Only students with verified college emails can join their community
- **Safe environment**: Built-in content moderation and reporting tools
- **Modern experience**: Fast, responsive, and mobile-friendly interface

<Note>
  Echo is designed for educational purposes and to foster positive community engagement. All users are expected to follow community guidelines and use the platform responsibly.
</Note>
